Mausoleum of Abu yanur Al-Dukali

The mausoleum is located in one of the largest historical cemeteries in the city, the cemetery of Sidi Bennour, and the cemetery for the burial of the dead Muslims extends over an area of an estimated 14,515 square meters, and it was created with the center where the village of Abu askaoun was an ancient cemetery dispensed with, and about two kilometers from the mausoleum there is the dome of Sidi Abi science, asceticism and righteousness in the region, where he migrated to it from the city of meshtaray or (mashanzaya) in the middle of the sixth century AH and settled in the village where people attached to him and loved him They honored him and stayed there until he died, where he was buried. The old mausoleum was demolished and rebuilt by Sultan Sidi Mohammed bin Abdullah and improvements were made by the commander si Rahal bin Yaro, who ruled the tribe of the Sons of Jaber during the reign of Moulay Hassan i, and by Si Ahmed al-Sisi from the dignitaries of the Muslim sons, on the sides of the mausoleum of the good Wali Sidi Abi Al-Nur.

Sidi Bennour municipal park

Sidi Bennour municipal park or Sidi Bennour swimming pool is the first municipal park of its kind in the Sidi Bennour region, where the launch of this large entertainment project was given in the presence of the head of the Sidi Bennour community, a number of members of the governing Bureau, the leader of the first district, a representative of the Regional Council, a large number of representatives of civil society, educational and cultural events in the city, representatives of the media and a number of citizens.

Zarqatouni garden

The history of the establishment of the zarqtouni park or the big park, as the residents of Sidi Bennour call it, also called the Sidi abalnour Arab Park[2], dates back to the beginning of the last century, especially after the entry of French colonization, by building the first European neighborhood, but it was demolished and replaced by a modern park.the commencement of construction works comes after deliberating on it during the extraordinary session of the Urban Council, which was held on November 26, 2010, and the Regional Council allocated an amount of 300,000,000 million dirhams in order to prepare the park.

Cultural Center

The Sidi Bennour Cultural Center is a cultural center in the city of Sidi Bennour, dakala Abda region, Morocco[1]. Today, the center is highly appreciated and respected by Arab intellectuals, creators and readers at all cultural and intellectual levels, because publishing a creative or cultural work bearing the center's mark earns it and its owner the favor of the cover and the value of the content.. In each session, the Arab Cultural Center creates an event inside the Great Hall of the exhibition through its new editions or signature concerts, for which some symbols of Arab culture are invited, both from inside Morocco and from abroad.

Mosque of Mohammed V

The mosque of Mohammed V (the cemetery mosque) is one of the modern Sidi benour mosques, located near Sidi Bennour. The name of the mosque is derived from" Mohammed V ibn Yusuf", one of the late kings of Morocco.

kasbah boulaouane

Kasbah Boulaouane is a kasbah in the town of Boulaouane, Morocco. It is situated on the rock which sits at the curve of Oum Er-Rbia River, overlooking the surrounding area. In the 20th century, an orientalist Edmond Doutté had provided a detailed description for the kasbah.

Sidi Smail

Sidi Smail is a small town and rural commune in El Jadida Province of the Casablanca-Settat region of Morocco. At the time of the 2004 census, the commune had a total population of 24,569 people living in 4,264 households.
